Trust scores for agents your customers rely on

Trust Auditor is an A2A-native API that combines identity signals (optional registry quorum) with behavioral history (transaction outcomes) to produce a trust score, status, and structured evidence—before you route money or sensitive work to another agent.

Who it’s for: teams building agent marketplaces, B2B agent platforms, or internal treasury / risk gates that need a consistent, explainable trust layer—not a black-box score.

View pricing Source & docs

Product truth: Reputation numbers come from your (or your partners’) transaction logs when you connect an MCP-style history service. Without that, the stack can run in demo / stub mode—fine for integration tests, not for production buyer decisions. See Getting started (configure config.js with your GitHub URL).

How it works

  1. Ingest outcomes — Your platform records success, failure, refunded, etc., into the transaction log (or compatible MCP sources).
  2. Audit on demand — Call the auditor with target_agent_id and context; optional registries attest identity.
  3. Decide with evidence — You get a trust score, APPROVED / FLAGGED / REVIEW_REQUIRED, warnings, and a payload suitable for policy engines.
